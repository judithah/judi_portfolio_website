The Beauty Industry & The "Luxury Marker" — Exploratory Data Analysis

Does paying more mean getting better? We put that assumption to the test.

Using data from 900+ cosmetics products across 50+ brands, my team analyzed whether price, green marketing claims, and product copy length are meaningful signals of quality or just noise.

What we found challenged industry intuition: ratings barely move across price tiers (r = 0.019), green labeling is most concentrated in budget products rather than luxury ones, and luxury brands actually use shorter descriptions, letting brand equity do the talking while mid-market brands compensate with copy complexity.

The real driver of price? Brand positioning, not ratings or word count. Adding brand-level features to our regression model jumped R² from 0.081 to 0.668.

Tools used: Python (Pandas, NumPy), SQL, API data extraction, linear regression, classification modeling
